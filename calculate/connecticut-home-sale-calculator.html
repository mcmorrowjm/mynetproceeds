About selling in Connecticut

At a glance: Capital gains tax ~ 6.99%; Transfer/Conveyance taxes: 0.75-1.25%; Typical seller closing costs: 1.5-2.5%. Median home price around $380,000 with average days on market near 35. Major markets include Hartford, New Haven, Stamford, Bridgeport.

Connecticut's top rate of 6.99% on capital gains, combined with high property taxes, can reduce net proceeds. However, proximity to New York City keeps Fairfield County values strong.

Market insight: Coastal Fairfield County benefits from NYC proximity while Hartford and New Haven offer more affordable options. The pandemic drove demand for Connecticut suburbs.

Tax note: Connecticut taxes capital gains as ordinary income with rates from 3% to 6.99% based on income and filing status.

Tips to maximize your net proceeds

  • Confirm eligibility for the 2-year primary residence exclusion ($250k single / $500k married).
  • Track improvements that raise your basis (roof, additions, HVAC).
  • Compare listing agents and negotiate commissions.
  • Address high-impact repairs prior to inspection to avoid large concessions.
