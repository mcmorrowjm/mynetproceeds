About selling in Florida

At a glance: Capital gains tax ~ 0%; Transfer/Conveyance taxes: $0.70 per $100; Typical seller closing costs: 1-2%. Median home price around $400,000 with average days on market near 35. Major markets include Miami, Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville.

Florida's zero state income tax means no state capital gains taxes on your home sale - a massive advantage that can save tens of thousands compared to high-tax states. This tax benefit, combined with strong demand from retirees and remote workers, makes Florida one of the best states for maximizing home sale proceeds.

Market insight: Miami continues seeing international investment, Tampa Bay booms with tech migration, and Orlando benefits from tourism recovery. The pandemic accelerated Florida's population growth.

Tax note: While there's no income tax, Florida charges documentary stamp taxes of $0.70 per $100 of sale price, plus additional stamps in Miami-Dade County.

Tips to maximize your net proceeds

  • Confirm eligibility for the 2-year primary residence exclusion ($250k single / $500k married).
  • Track improvements that raise your basis (roof, additions, HVAC).
  • Compare listing agents and negotiate commissions.
  • Address high-impact repairs prior to inspection to avoid large concessions.
