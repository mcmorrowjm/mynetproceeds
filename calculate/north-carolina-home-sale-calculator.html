About selling in North Carolina

At a glance: Capital gains tax ~ 4.5%; Transfer/Conveyance taxes: $1 per $500; Typical seller closing costs: 1-2%. Median home price around $340,000 with average days on market near 28. Major markets include Charlotte, Raleigh, Greensboro, Durham.

North Carolina's flat 4.5% tax on capital gains is competitive for the Southeast. The Research Triangle and Charlotte continue attracting tech and finance workers.

Market insight: Raleigh-Durham tech boom continues. Charlotte's banking sector drives demand. Asheville attracts retirees while Outer Banks commands beach premiums.

Tax note: Recent flat tax implementation simplified calculations and reduced rates for many sellers.

Tips to maximize your net proceeds

  • Confirm eligibility for the 2-year primary residence exclusion ($250k single / $500k married).

  • Compare listing agents and negotiate commissions.
  • Address high-impact repairs prior to inspection to avoid large concessions.
