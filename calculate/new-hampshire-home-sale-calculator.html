About selling in New Hampshire

At a glance: Capital gains tax ~ 0%; Transfer/Conveyance taxes: $0.75 per $100; Typical seller closing costs: 1-2%. Median home price around $450,000 with average days on market near 30. Major markets include Manchester, Nashua, Concord, Portsmouth.

New Hampshire has no state income tax on wages or capital gains, though it taxes investment income. The Boston commuter corridor commands premium prices.

Market insight: Southern NH benefits from Boston proximity without Massachusetts taxes. Seacoast and Lakes Region attract vacation buyers.

Tax note: No income or sales tax but high property taxes. 5% tax on interest and dividends doesn't apply to capital gains.

Tips to maximize your net proceeds

  • Confirm eligibility for the 2-year primary residence exclusion ($250k single / $500k married).
  • Track improvements that raise your basis (roof, additions, HVAC).
  • Compare listing agents and negotiate commissions.
  • Address high-impact repairs prior to inspection to avoid large concessions.
