The Hidden Cost: Capital Gains Taxes Explained

Capital gains taxes are the government's share of your profit. If you've lived in your home 2 of the last 5 years, you may exclude $250,000 (single) or $500,000 (married filing jointly). After exclusions, remaining gains are taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% federally depending on income, plus any state tax.

Investment Properties & Rentals

Investment properties face depreciation recapture—prior depreciation is taxed at 25% when you sell. Our calculator estimates this for you and applies state tax proxies unless you override them.
